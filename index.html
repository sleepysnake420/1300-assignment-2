CSCI 1300 : Responsive Redesign

Overview

The goal of this assignment is to practice the workflow of redesigning a simple website. Here I analyze and identify flaws in an existing interface, create low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes for various screen sizes, and build a responsive website based on those prototypes.

Identifying Usability Problems

The website I have chosen to redesign is The Wild Colonial Tavern.
Click here to see the website

Since the Wild Colonial Tavern is one of my favorite bars in Providence but also has a highly unresponsive and badly designed website, I chose that page as my example. The image is simply chopped/overflows on resize, and is arbitrarily positioned at the center because it does not adapt to the size of the screen. You may easily overlook the various menu items because they are so tiny and have such a weak contrast to the backdrop color. There is a lot of room for redesigning this page.

Problems

  • Homepage badly designed with a huge image at the center but very small and low contrast menu items that a user could miss
  • Image does not scale with screen size and instead overflows
  • Bad or insufficient use of margins, padding
  • Bad naming and usage of menu items (One of them just says I-phone and it located before the Home menu item)
  • Huge gap above the photo and below the contact information for no reason
  • No icon next to tab title
  • Website is insecure (http not https)
  • Lighthouse analysis fails indicating that certain key features or elements are missing from the website code
  • Learnability and memorability is low as the menu items are not standard and are verbose
  • Footer could be implemented to house contact information
  • Navbar could be implemented to better display the menu items

Accessibility

  • Language missing or Invalid
  • No heading structure
  • No page regions / ARIA markers



The three problems mentioned above were found by the WAVE evaluation tool. I concur with their conclusions. Due to "lingo" or informal terminology used in menu items, some people, especially those who come from outside of this region, may not comprehend it. The absence of headings on the website compromises its usability and readability. Additionally, the absence of region or ARIA indicators makes it impossible for screen readers to understand what is happening on the website, excluding users with visual impairments. Additionally, although though WAVE did not notice the contrast problems, I think they are present on the website and need to be manually checked.
